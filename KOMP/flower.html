The Valley of Flowers, India

This UNESCO World Heritage site in Uttarakhand is popular for not only its scenic beauty, but also for a mesmerising valley of vibrant flowers amid majestic mountains.

Lavender fields, France

When the fields of Provence in France don lavender carpets during summers, there is no sight in the world to match its postcard-like visuals.

Rose Valley, Bulgaria

A rose brought from Syria turned into a symbol of Bulgaria, making it one of the biggest rose-oil producers in the world. An apt spot if your senses seek a surreal experience and unforgettable sights.

Cherry blossoms, Japan

The country patiently waits, all year, for these to bloom. One of the major highlights of Japan, these flowers steal the show when they come into bloom.

Mexican sunflower fields, Thailand

Blooming only in the month of November and December, wild sunflowers paint the valleys in bright gold colour, and the sight is a treat to sore eyes.

iStock

Heather flowers, Scotland

The peak blooming season is in late summers, when the slopes of Petland Hills turn into mauve and purple shades. As a bonus, the scents of these flowers will guide you through the trails.

iStock

Lupin blooms, New Zealand

One of the best times to visit New Zealand is during November and mid-January, during the Lupin flowering season when these stunning flowers put up a spectacular show.

iStock

Keukenhof Tulip Garden, Holland

Touted as one of the largest flower gardens in the world, it has gorgeous views of blooming tulips as the main highlight.

iStock

Pink rhododendron blooms, Romania

These bright pink, perfumed flowers prefer to grow in the mountainous meadows of Romania. The entire landscape turns absolutely stunning and is worth a visit.
